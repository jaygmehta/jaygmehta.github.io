The number of group homomorphisms from $\mathbb Z_{10}$ to $\mathbb Z_{20}$ is

(a) zero<br>
(b) one<br>
(c) five<br>
(d) ten<br>

**Solution**

$o(\phi(1)) \mid o(1)$ and hence $o(\phi(1))\mid \gcd(10,20) = 10$. Therefore, $o(\phi(1)) = 1,2,5,10$.\\
If $o(\phi(1)) = 1$, then $\phi(1) = 0$ which gives $phi(x) = 0$ for all $x \in \mathbb Z_{10}$.\\
If $o(\phi(1)) = 2$, then $\phi(1) =10 $ which gives $phi(x) = 10x$ for all $x \in \mathbb Z_{10}$.\\
If $o(\phi(1)) = 5$, then $\phi(1) = 4,8,12,16$ which gives $phi(x) = 4x, 8x,12x, 16x$ for all $x \in \mathbb Z_{10}$.\\
If $o(\phi(1)) = 10$, then $\phi(1) =2,6,10,14,18 $ which gives $phi(x) = 2x,6x,10x,14x,18 x$ for all $x \in \mathbb Z_{10}$.\\
Hence, there are total $10$ homomorphisms from $\mathbb Z_{10}$ to $\mathbb Z_{20}$.
