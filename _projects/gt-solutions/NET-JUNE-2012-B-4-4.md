The last digit of $(38)^{2011}$ is 

(a) $6$<br>
(b) $2$<br>
(c) $4$<br>
(d) $8$<br>

**Solution**

Last digit means remainder obtained on dividing the number by $10$. Now, $38 \equiv 8 \pmod{10}$. So it is equivalent to find the last digit of $8^{2011}$.\\
Apply Euler's theorem, to get the answer $2$.
