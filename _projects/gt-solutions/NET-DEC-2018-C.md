**Solution**

(a) **True**. If $G$ is finite then $A(G)$ is finite. Aut($G$) is a subgroup of $A(G)$ and hence it is finite.<br>

(b) **False**. Let $\phi : \mathbb Z_8 \to \mathbb Z_8$ be a automorphism. Then $o(\phi(1))=8$ and hence $\phi(1)=1,3,5,7$. If $\phi(1)=1$, then it is identity. If $\phi(1)=3$, then $\phi(x)=3x$. Now, $\phi^2(x) = \phi(\phi(x)) = \phi(3x) = 9x = x \pmod 8$. Thus, $\phi^2=I$ (identity). Similarly, if $\phi(x) = 5x$ or $7x$, then $\phi^2 = I$ (identity). Thus, Aut$(\mathbb Z_8)$ has $4$ elements, i.e. a group of order $4$ in which every element has order $2$. Therefore, Aut$(\mathbb Z_8) \approx K_4$, which is not cyclic.<br>

(c) **False**. $G = \mathbb Z$. Then Aut($\mathbb Z$) is isomorphic to $\mathbb Z_2$. It has only two automorphisms viz. identity $f(1) = 1$ and an automorphism of order $2$ which is $g(1) = -1$.<br>

(d) **False**. Aut$(\mathbb Z) \approx \mathbb Z_2 \approx \text{Aut}(\mathbb Z_3)$ but $\mathbb Z \not\approx \mathbb Z_3$.
