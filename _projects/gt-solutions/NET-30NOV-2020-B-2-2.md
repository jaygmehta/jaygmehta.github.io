The last two digits of $3^{2019}$ are

(a) $27$<br>
(b) $37$<br>
(c) $57$<br>
(d) $67$<br>

**Solution**

To obtain last two digits of a number, we need to compute the remainder obtained by dividing the number by $100$.\\
By Euler's theorem, if $a$ is relatively prime to $n$, then $a^{\phi(n)}\equiv 1 \pmod n$. Here $a=3$ and $n=100$. Since $(3,100)=1$ and $\phi(100)=\phi(4)\phi(25)=2 \times 20 = 40$, we have
$$\begin{aligned}
& 3^{40} &\equiv &\ 1 \pmod {100}\\
\Rightarrow & {(3^{40})}^{20} &\equiv &\ 1^{20} \pmod {100}\\
\Rightarrow & 3^{2000}&\equiv &\ 1 \pmod {100}\\
\Rightarrow & 3^{2019}&\equiv &\ 3^{19} \pmod {100}.
\end{aligned}$$
If suffices to find last two digits of $3^{19}$. Note that $3^6 = 729 \equiv 29 \pmod{100}$. So $3^{18} = 29^3 = 24389 \equiv 89 \pmod{100}$. Hence, $3^{19} = 89 \times 3 = 267 \equiv 67 \pmod{100}$.
