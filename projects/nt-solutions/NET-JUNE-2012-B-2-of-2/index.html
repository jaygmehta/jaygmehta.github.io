NET JUNE 2012 (B)

The number of positive divisors of $50000$ is

(a) $20$.
(b) $30$.
(c) $40$.
(d) $50$.

Solution

If $n={p_1}^{\alpha_1} {p_2}^{\alpha_2} \cdots {p_k}^{\alpha_k}$ and $d \mid n$ then $d$ is of the form $d = {p_1}^{\beta_1} {p_2}^{\beta_2} \cdots {p_k}^{\beta_k}$, where $0 \le \beta_i \le \alpha_i$ for all $i = 1,2,\ldots,k$. Thus, the number of positive divisors of $n$ is $(\alpha_1+1)(\alpha_2 +1)\cdots (\alpha_k+1)$.

Here, $50000= 2^4 \times 5^5$ and therefore the number of positive divisors of $50000$ is $(4+1)(5+1) = 5\cdot 6 =30$.
