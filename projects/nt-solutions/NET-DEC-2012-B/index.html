NET DEC 2012 (B)

The last two digits of $7^{81}$ are

(a) $07$.
(b) $17$.
(c) $37$.
(d) $47$.

Solution

To obtain last two digits of a number, we need to compute the remainder obtained by dividing the number by $100$.

By Euler’s theorem, if $a$ is relatively prime to $n$, then $a^{\phi(n)}\equiv 1 \pmod n$. Here $a=7$ and $n=100$. Since $(7,100)=1$ and $\phi(100)=\phi(4)\phi(25)=2 \times 20 = 40$, we have

\[\begin{align*} & 7^{40} &\equiv &\ 1 \pmod {100}\\ \Rightarrow & {(7^{40})}^{2} &\equiv &\ 1^{2} \pmod {100}\\ \Rightarrow & 7 \cdot 7^{80} = 7^{81} &\equiv &\ 7 \cdot 1= 7 \pmod {100}. \end{align*}\]

Thus, last two digits (i.e. the remainder) is $07$.
